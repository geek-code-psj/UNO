üé¨ Game Setup

  1. Shuffle the entire 108-card deck thoroughly.
  2. Deal 7 cards face-down to each player.
  3. Place the remaining cards face-down as the draw pile.
  4. Flip the top card of the draw pile to start the discard pile.
  5. If the first card is a Wild Draw Four, reshuffle and reflip.
  6. If the first card is any other action card, its effect applies to the first player.
  7. The player to the left of the dealer goes first (clockwise).
üí° First Card Rules: If a Skip is flipped, the first player is skipped. If a Reverse is flipped, the dealer goes first instead. If a Draw Two is flipped, the first player draws 2 and loses their turn. If a Wild is flipped, the first player chooses the color.

üéÆ How to Play

On your turn, you must play a card that matches the top card of the discard pile by one of these criteria:

  • Same Color ‚Äî Any card matching the active color (e.g., red on red)
  • Same Number ‚Äî A number card matching the number (e.g., Blue 7 on Red 7)
  • Same Symbol ‚Äî An action card matching the type (e.g., Skip on Skip, regardless of color)
  • Wild Card ‚Äî Can always be played regardless of what's on top

If You Can't Play

  1. Draw one card from the draw pile.
  2. If the drawn card can be played, you may play it immediately.
  3. If not (or you choose not to), your turn ends.
‚ö†Ô∏è Draw Pile Empty? When the draw pile runs out, the discard pile (except the top card) is shuffled and becomes the new draw pile. Play continues normally.

‚ö° Power Cards ‚Äî Complete Guide

Power cards (also called action cards) are the heart of UNO strategy. Here are the complete official rules for each:

‚õî Skip Card 20 points

Effect: The next player in turn order is completely skipped. They do not play or draw. Play passes to the player after them.

When to play: Match by color, or play on another Skip card of any color.

Strategy tip: Save Skip cards to block opponents who are close to winning (have 1-2 cards left). Playing a Skip when the next player has 1 card prevents them from going out!

üîÑ Reverse Card 20 points

Effect: Reverses the direction of play. If play was going clockwise, it now goes counter-clockwise, and vice versa.

2-Player Special Rule: In a 2-player game, Reverse acts exactly like a Skip card ‚Äî the other player loses their turn and you play again.

When to play: Match by color, or play on another Reverse card of any color.

Strategy tip: Use Reverse strategically to give yourself more turns or to prevent a dangerous player from playing. In 2-player games, Reverse is essentially a free extra turn!

üì• Draw Two (+2) Card 20 points

Effect: The next player must draw 2 cards from the draw pile AND lose their turn. They cannot play any card that turn.

When to play: Match by color, or play on another Draw Two card of any color.

‚ö†Ô∏è No Stacking! According to official UNO rules, you CANNOT stack a +2 on top of another +2 to make the next player draw 4. The targeted player must draw 2 and lose their turn. No exceptions.

Strategy tip: This is your best defensive card against someone about to win. Force them to draw 2 cards and they're back in the game!

üåà Wild Card 50 points

Effect: Can be played on any card, regardless of color or number. When played, the player declares the next color (red, blue, green, or yellow). The next player must match this chosen color (unless they play another Wild).

When to play: Anytime ‚Äî there are no restrictions. Wild cards are always legal to play.

Strategy tip: Choose the color you have the most of in your hand. This maximizes your chances of playing on subsequent turns. Save Wilds for when you're stuck with no color matches, or when you want to strategically switch to a color that benefits you.

üíÄ Wild Draw Four (+4) Card 50 points

Effect: The most powerful card in UNO! When played:

  1. The player declares the next color
  2. The next player must draw 4 cards from the draw pile
  3. The next player loses their turn

The Legality Rule

A Wild Draw Four can officially only be played when you have no cards matching the current active color. You may have cards of other colors, numbers, or other action cards ‚Äî that's fine. The restriction is specifically about the active color.

The Challenge Mechanic

If a player suspects the Wild Draw Four was played illegally (the player had a card of the matching color), they can challenge:

  • Challenge succeeds (player DID have a matching color card): The player who played the +4 must draw 4 cards instead. The challenger does NOT draw.
  • Challenge fails (player had NO matching color cards): The challenger draws 6 cards (the original 4 + 2 penalty for a failed challenge) and loses their turn.
üí° Bluffing is allowed! You CAN play a Wild Draw Four even if you have a matching color ‚Äî you're just bluffing. If nobody challenges, you get away with it. This adds mind-game strategy to UNO!

Strategy tip: The +4 is devastating. Save it for critical moments ‚Äî blocking someone about to win, or when you desperately need a color change. If you're bluffing, play confidently and hope they don't challenge!

üéØ Calling UNO

One of UNO's most iconic and important rules:

  • When you play your second-to-last card (going down to 1 card), you must call "UNO!"
  • You should call UNO as you play the card, not after
  • If another player catches you with 1 card without having called UNO, you must draw 2 penalty cards
  • The catch must happen before the next player takes their turn
  • If you are not caught before the next player plays, you're safe
üí° AI Bot Behavior: In offline mode, Easy bots sometimes "forget" to call UNO (30% chance), Medium bots always remember, and Hard bots always call UNO plus watch for your mistakes!

‚ùå Common Rule Misconceptions

Many house rules are popular but NOT in the official UNO rules:

‚ùå Stacking +2 on +2: You CANNOT play a +2 when someone plays a +2 on you to make the next player draw 4. You must draw 2 and lose your turn. This has been confirmed by Mattel (UNO's manufacturer) multiple times.
‚ùå Stacking +4 on +4: Same rule ‚Äî you cannot stack Wild Draw Fours. The targeted player draws 4 and loses their turn. No chain reactions.
‚ùå Draw Until You Can Play: You only draw ONE card. If it can be played, you MAY play it. If not, your turn ends. You do NOT keep drawing until you find a playable card.
‚ùå Playing Multiple Cards: You can only play ONE card per turn, even if you have multiple valid plays.
üí° UNO Arena uses official rules! Our game engine enforces all the proper official rules listed above. This ensures fair, consistent gameplay for everyone.

üß† Strategy Tips

  1. Count cards: Pay attention to how many cards each opponent has. Target players close to winning with +2 and Skip cards.
  2. Control color: Switch to colors where you have the most cards. This gives you more playable options.
  3. Save Wilds: Don't waste Wild cards early. They're your safety net when you're stuck.
  4. Dump high-value cards: Get rid of +2, Skip, Reverse, and especially Wilds early to minimize the points you'll give opponents if they win.
  5. Watch for UNO calls: If an opponent has 2 cards and plays one without calling UNO, challenge them immediately for a 2-card penalty!
  6. Bluff with +4: Playing a Wild Draw Four when you have matching colors is risky but can be devastating. The 6-card penalty for a failed challenge deters most challengers.
  7. Reverse in 2-player: In 2-player games, Reverse is essentially a Skip ‚Äî giving you an extra turn. Use it aggressively!
  8. Track colors: If an opponent keeps drawing, they likely don't have the current active color. Keep playing that color to force them to keep drawing!
